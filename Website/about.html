Founder, Designer & Engineer at Elevon

I'm a Duke engineering student, competitive cyclist, and the sole creator behind every Elevon map. What started as a personal project to commemorate my favorite trails has grown into a one-person business dedicated to turning meaningful places into lasting art.

My Story

The idea for Elevon was born during my high school racing days with NICA Arkansas. Racing hours from home meant I rarely had the chance to pre-ride courses like local athletes. To level the playing field, I started creating 3D models of race courses and printing them out.

Having a physical map in my hands gave me true understanding of the terrainâ€”where the climbs were positioned, how descents flowed, and how features connected. What began as a personal training tool has evolved into something bigger: turning meaningful places into lasting art.

Running Elevon solo means I'm involved in every step. I personally design each map, optimize it for printing, and package it for shipping. This isn't just a businessâ€”it's my way of celebrating the cycling community that shaped who I am today.
